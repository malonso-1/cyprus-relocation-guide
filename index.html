Step-by-step guide to moving to Cyprus: visas, housing, healthcare, and settling in

Planning to move to Cyprus? This guide covers everything from visa applications to finding a home and setting up your life on the island. All information sourced from Cyprus Tax Life, a resource built by expats who made the move themselves.

Step 1: Choose Your City

Cyprus offers several distinct cities, each with its own character and cost of living. Here is what to expect:

LarnacaMost affordable coastal city. Average EUR 1,470/month. International airport, beach lifestyle, growing expat scene.
LimassolBusiness hub. Average EUR 1,800/month. Best nightlife, most international, but higher rents.
NicosiaCapital city. Government offices, universities, divided city with unique character.
PaphosWestern coast. Popular with British retirees, archaeological sites, relaxed pace.

Step 2: Understand Your Visa Options

Your visa depends on your nationality and work situation. EU citizens have it easiest, but non-EU workers have several pathways too.

  • Full Visa Guide Overview of all visa categories for Cyprus.
  • Digital Nomad Visa For remote workers earning EUR 3,500+/month. Valid up to 3 years.
  • Work Permit Employer-sponsored or as company director. Requires job offer.
  • Self-Employed Visa For freelancers and solo entrepreneurs. Sole trader vs Ltd routes.
  • Permanent Residence Three pathways: EU registration, investment, or 5-year continuous residence.
  • Yellow Slip (MEU1) EU citizens: your first registration step. Processing takes 2-4 weeks.

Healthcare in Cyprus

Cyprus runs the GHS (GESY), a universal healthcare system funded by contributions. As a tax resident, you are automatically enrolled.

  • GHS Contribution Rates 2.65% on dividends (capped), plus employer/employee contributions on salary.
